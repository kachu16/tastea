Brewing Guide

Step 1:

Boil 180-225ml of water up to 90°C. This means that the water should appear to be losing its air in a mild form and is in a state before the water rolling water, say, mild simmer.

Step 2:

Keep two grams of tea leaves in a tea pot (or a glass vessel). Pour the water boiled in this pot. Let it stay for three minutes.

Step 3:

Strain the tea in a tea cup. Enjoy the flavour and aroma of the mountains from the first sip.

Important Precautions

  • Warm the tea pot at step 2. No !! Do not put it on the stove, but you can just warm it slightly by inserting the tea pot or the brewing pot in warm water for a few minutes.
  • Use fresh water and put it to boil. Please do not use water that has already been boiled or that was kept uncovered. It affects the taste of the tea.
  • Two grams of tea leaves is a spoonful of tea. One can add or reduce the quantity as per individual preferences.

Darjeeling Tea is best enjoyed in a way of individual preferences. Feel free to mend the stated guide to get to suite your palate.
